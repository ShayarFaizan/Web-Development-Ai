How to Make Your Brand Go Viral with Short Videos

Tips and strategies for creating short-form video content that captures attention and drives engagement in 2024.

By Marketing Club Team · June 2025

Short-form videos are dominating social media feeds and changing the way brands connect with audiences. With platforms like TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts, even small brands can reach millions—if they know how to capture attention fast. Here's how you can make your brand go viral with short videos in 2024.

1. Hook Viewers in the First 3 Seconds

Attention spans are shorter than ever. Start your video with a bold visual, a surprising statement, or a question that makes viewers stop scrolling. The first few seconds decide whether someone will keep watching or swipe away.

Example: A beauty brand starts with "Stop! You're applying your foundation wrong—here's why." Instant curiosity, instant engagement.

2. Keep It Short, Punchy, and Relatable

Aim for 15–30 seconds. Focus on one idea per video. Use humor, trending sounds, or relatable situations to connect with your audience. Authentic, less-polished videos often perform better than over-produced ones.

Pro Tip: Use captions and on-screen text—most people watch with the sound off!

3. Leverage Trends and Challenges

Jump on trending audio, hashtags, or challenges relevant to your brand. This increases your chances of being discovered by new audiences. But always add your unique twist to stand out from the crowd.

Example: A fitness brand joins a trending dance challenge but adds workout moves to make it their own.

4. Show Behind-the-Scenes & User Stories

People love authenticity. Share behind-the-scenes clips, day-in-the-life moments, or real customer stories. User-generated content (UGC) builds trust and encourages more people to engage with your brand.

Example: Share a customer's reaction video or a quick "how we make it" clip from your workspace.

5. End with a Strong Call to Action

Tell viewers exactly what you want them to do next—like, share, comment, follow, or visit your website. Make your CTA clear, direct, and easy to act on.

Pro Tip: Use on-screen graphics or text to reinforce your CTA visually.

Conclusion: Ready to Go Viral?

Going viral isn't just luck—it's about understanding your audience, staying on top of trends, and delivering value in every second. Start experimenting, track your analytics, and don't be afraid to show your brand's personality. The next viral video could be yours!
